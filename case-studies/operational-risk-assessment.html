Operational Risk Assessment

Internal process review focused on identifying workflow risks, control gaps, and operational exposure points.

Objective

Evaluate internal operating processes to identify key risks, control weaknesses, and areas for mitigation.

Context

Reflects enterprise and operational risk work aligned with risk register management, RCSA-style thinking, and mitigation planning across institutional processes.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • Which processes carry the highest operational risk?
  • What control gaps increase exposure?
  • Who owns the key risks and follow-up actions?
  • How can targeted mitigation reduce exposure over time?

Methods Used

  • Risk assessment
  • RCSA-style control review
  • Process walkthroughs
  • Mitigation planning
  • Risk register analysis

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Number of high-priority risks identified
  • Risk mitigation completion rate
  • Residual risk trend over time
  • Open risk actions by age

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Executive leadership
  • Operations teams
  • Risk and compliance stakeholders
  • Department leaders

Estimated Timeline

Approximately 4–6 weeks.

Key Findings

  • Risk ownership was not consistently assigned
  • Control execution varied by workflow
  • Issue visibility depended on informal escalation

Recommendations

  • Assign ownership for major operational risks
  • Standardize key controls across processes
  • Use centralized issue tracking and reporting

Skills Demonstrated

Operational risk assessment, control review, workflow analysis, and mitigation planning.
