Department Risk Mapping

Identification of operational dependencies and exposure points across departments in higher education settings.

Objective

Map departmental dependencies and identify where concentration, handoff, or communication risks affect operations.

Context

Reflects cross-functional risk analysis where multiple departments contribute to shared services, compliance obligations, or student-facing operations.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • Which departments depend most heavily on single roles or processes?
  • Where do weak handoffs create operational exposure?
  • Which dependencies could interrupt service or compliance performance?
  • How should interdepartmental risks be escalated?

Methods Used

  • Dependency mapping
  • Risk identification workshops
  • Process walkthroughs
  • Escalation and control review

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Number of single-point dependencies identified
  • Open high-impact interdepartmental risks
  • Cross-functional escalation response time
  • Mitigation completion rate

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Department leadership
  • Academic and administrative staff
  • Operations and compliance teams
  • Executive leadership

Estimated Timeline

Approximately 4–6 weeks.

Key Findings

  • Some critical workflows depended on single individuals
  • Cross-department handoffs were weakly documented
  • Escalation practices varied by function

Recommendations

  • Map dependencies between operating teams
  • Cross-train critical roles
  • Standardize interdepartmental escalation procedures

Skills Demonstrated

Risk mapping, dependency analysis, workflow review, and resilience planning.
