Control Monitoring

Assessment of issue tracking, exception handling, and operational control quality in finance settings.

Objective

Review how controls are monitored over time and how exceptions are identified, assessed, and escalated.

Context

Reflects control testing and monitoring work that supports stronger reporting quality, issue visibility, and remediation discipline.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • Which controls require periodic monitoring or testing?
  • Where do exceptions recur or remain unresolved?
  • Are monitoring thresholds clear and actionable?
  • What evidence demonstrates effective control performance?

Methods Used

  • Control monitoring review
  • Issue and exception analysis
  • Root-cause review
  • Evidence and testing assessment

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Exception volume and aging
  • Control pass / fail rate
  • Root-cause recurrence rate
  • Remediation completion timeliness

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Finance operations
  • Risk and control owners
  • Leadership reviewers
  • Audit and compliance stakeholders

Estimated Timeline

Approximately 3–5 weeks.

Key Findings

  • Monitoring thresholds were not consistently defined
  • Exception reviews lacked standard root-cause analysis
  • Evidence of monitoring varied in quality

Recommendations

  • Define thresholds for monitoring and escalation
  • Standardize exception review procedures
  • Align documentation requirements across teams

Skills Demonstrated

Control testing, exception monitoring, issue analysis, and operational reporting review.
