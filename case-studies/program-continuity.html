Program Continuity

Assessment of disruption response planning and continuity strategies supporting ongoing services, critical operations, and institutional resilience.

Objective

Review readiness for operational disruption and evaluate service continuity strategies for critical functions.

Context

This work aligns with continuity planning efforts used in institutions where disruptions affect public access, program delivery, safety, or compliance obligations.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • Which functions are critical to maintain during disruption?
  • What dependencies create the greatest continuity risk?
  • Where are backup processes missing or weak?
  • How quickly could key operations recover?

Methods Used

  • Business continuity review
  • Dependency mapping
  • Scenario analysis
  • Control and process review
  • Recovery planning assessment

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Coverage of continuity plans for critical services
  • Recovery time expectations by function
  • Number of single-point dependencies identified
  • Testing frequency for continuity procedures
  • Operational downtime during disruption events

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Executive leadership
  • Program managers
  • Operations teams
  • Public safety and facilities
  • Communications and stakeholder-facing teams

Estimated Timeline

Approximately 4–8 weeks depending on the number of critical functions reviewed.

Key Findings

  • Continuity plans were incomplete
  • Operational dependencies were undocumented
  • Response procedures varied across teams

Recommendations

  • Create continuity playbooks
  • Document operational dependencies
  • Conduct disruption response exercises

Skills Demonstrated

Business continuity planning, operational risk assessment, resilience strategy analysis, and dependency mapping.
