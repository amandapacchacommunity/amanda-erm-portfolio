Business Continuity

Review of resilience planning and disruption-readiness practices supporting operational continuity.

Objective

Assess continuity readiness and identify improvements that support faster recovery and stronger operational resilience.

Context

Reflects continuity planning and resilience review work where critical services, recovery priorities, and operational dependencies must be defined clearly.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • Which operations must be restored first after disruption?
  • What dependencies threaten recovery speed?
  • Where are backup processes or plans missing?
  • How prepared are teams for interruption scenarios?

Methods Used

  • Business continuity assessment
  • Dependency mapping
  • Recovery priority review
  • Scenario and testing review

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Recovery time targets by function
  • Continuity plan completion rate
  • Testing or exercise frequency
  • Coverage of critical-function owners

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Executive leadership
  • Operations and service teams
  • Facilities and safety stakeholders
  • Communications and support functions

Estimated Timeline

Approximately 4–8 weeks.

Key Findings

  • Critical process dependencies were not fully documented
  • Recovery priorities were unclear
  • Continuity testing was limited

Recommendations

  • Identify and document critical processes
  • Define recovery priorities and owners
  • Run continuity exercises on a routine basis

Skills Demonstrated

Continuity planning, resilience assessment, dependency mapping, and disruption response review.
