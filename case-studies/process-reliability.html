Process Reliability

Assessment of workflow strength, repeatability, and control effectiveness in finance operations.

Objective

Determine whether core workflows are reliable, repeatable, and supported by effective controls.

Context

Reflects operations and reporting improvement work focused on reducing manual variation, clarifying workflow design, and strengthening control-supported execution.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • Which workflow steps fail or vary most often?
  • Where do manual activities create inconsistency?
  • How do handoffs affect process quality?
  • What controls best support repeatable execution?

Methods Used

  • Workflow analysis
  • Process mapping
  • Control effectiveness review
  • Handoff and exception analysis

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Cycle time by process stage
  • Error or rework rate
  • Manual intervention frequency
  • Control completion rate

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Finance and operations staff
  • Process owners
  • Leadership reviewers
  • Control stakeholders

Estimated Timeline

Approximately 3–5 weeks.

Key Findings

  • Critical steps depended heavily on manual effort
  • Workflow documentation was inconsistent
  • Handoffs created avoidable delays and gaps

Recommendations

  • Document critical process steps clearly
  • Reduce unnecessary handoffs
  • Align controls directly to workflow stages

Skills Demonstrated

Workflow analysis, process reliability review, control effectiveness assessment, and operational improvement.
