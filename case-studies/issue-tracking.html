Issue Tracking

Review of operational issue monitoring, corrective actions, and accountability for resolution.

Objective

Improve issue visibility, corrective action discipline, and accountability for timely resolution.

Context

Reflects issue-management and remediation tracking work where documenting findings, owners, due dates, and evidence strengthens operational follow-through.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • How are issues captured, categorized, and prioritized?
  • Which issues remain open too long and why?
  • What evidence demonstrates effective remediation?
  • How should aging issues be escalated?

Methods Used

  • Issue log review
  • Corrective action tracking
  • Root-cause and trend analysis
  • Escalation and closure review

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Open issue aging
  • Remediation completion rate
  • Average time to closure
  • Repeat issue rate

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Operations leaders
  • Risk and compliance stakeholders
  • Issue owners and department managers
  • Executive oversight teams

Estimated Timeline

Approximately 2–4 weeks.

Key Findings

  • Issues were tracked in multiple locations
  • Closure evidence was inconsistent
  • Aging items were not escalated consistently

Recommendations

  • Centralize issue logs and reporting
  • Require documented evidence for closure
  • Escalate aging items based on defined thresholds

Skills Demonstrated

Issue management, corrective action tracking, operational reporting, and control follow-up review.
