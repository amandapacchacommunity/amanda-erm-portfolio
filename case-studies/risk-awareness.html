Risk Awareness

Review of control gaps, operational exposure, and resilience concerns across nonprofit functions.

Objective

Identify areas where operational risks are insufficiently monitored, understood, or communicated.

Context

Reflects risk identification and awareness-building practices used in organizations with limited resources, cross-functional dependencies, and evolving compliance expectations.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • Which risks are most visible, and which remain under-identified?
  • Are key control gaps being tracked consistently?
  • Who owns each operational or compliance risk?
  • How are emerging risks communicated to leadership?

Methods Used

  • Risk register review
  • Operational risk analysis
  • Control gap assessment
  • Stakeholder interviews and issue review

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Number of risks with assigned owners
  • Open high-priority risks
  • Risk review frequency
  • Mitigation completion rate

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Executive leadership
  • Program and operations leaders
  • Compliance and governance stakeholders

Estimated Timeline

Approximately 2–4 weeks.

Key Findings

  • Risk ownership was not clearly assigned
  • Cross-functional risks were not consistently tracked
  • Escalation triggers varied across processes

Recommendations

  • Assign ownership for key operational risks
  • Track risks using a central register
  • Clarify thresholds for escalation and review

Skills Demonstrated

Operational risk analysis, control gap assessment, resilience review, and risk communication planning.
