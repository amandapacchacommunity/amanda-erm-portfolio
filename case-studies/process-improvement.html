Process Improvement

Workflow analysis focused on efficiency, clarity, and execution quality across operational processes.

Objective

Identify process inefficiencies and improve execution quality through clearer workflows and stronger operational design.

Context

Reflects process analysis work used to strengthen service delivery, reduce rework, improve handoffs, and align operating practice to documented procedure.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • Which steps create the most delay or rework?
  • Where are roles unclear during handoffs?
  • What process changes would improve consistency and speed?
  • How can workflows better support accountability and quality?

Methods Used

  • Process mapping
  • Workflow redesign analysis
  • Handoff review
  • Operational documentation alignment

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Cycle time reduction
  • Rework or duplicate-step volume
  • Process completion timeliness
  • Stakeholder satisfaction with workflow clarity

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Operations teams
  • Department managers
  • Service-delivery staff
  • Leadership sponsors

Estimated Timeline

Approximately 3–6 weeks.

Key Findings

  • Duplicate steps increased delays
  • Role confusion affected workflow handoffs
  • Procedures did not always match actual practice

Recommendations

  • Simplify process steps and remove duplication
  • Clarify ownership for each workflow stage
  • Update procedures to reflect actual operations

Skills Demonstrated

Process mapping, workflow redesign, operational analysis, and execution improvement.
