Process Documentation

Review of workflows, policy support materials, and documentation needs that strengthen nonprofit operational consistency.

Objective

Improve operational clarity, reduce reliance on informal knowledge, and support consistent execution through better documentation.

Context

Reflects operational documentation and policy support work where process clarity improves continuity, onboarding, compliance readiness, and quality control.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • Which workflows are least documented but most critical?
  • Where do staff rely on informal knowledge instead of procedure?
  • What documentation gaps create compliance or quality risk?
  • How can process documentation improve repeatability and accountability?

Methods Used

  • Process mapping
  • Documentation review
  • Policy support analysis
  • Workflow standardization review

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Percentage of core processes documented
  • Procedure review and update frequency
  • Reduction in process ambiguity or rework
  • Onboarding efficiency for recurring tasks

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Operations staff
  • Program teams
  • Compliance stakeholders
  • Managers and new staff

Estimated Timeline

Approximately 3–6 weeks depending on the number of workflows documented.

Key Findings

  • Core procedures were not centrally maintained
  • Documentation was outdated in some areas
  • Ownership of process updates was unclear

Recommendations

  • Build a central process library
  • Assign document owners for maintenance
  • Review procedures on a regular schedule

Skills Demonstrated

Process design, workflow documentation, operational support planning, and policy alignment.
