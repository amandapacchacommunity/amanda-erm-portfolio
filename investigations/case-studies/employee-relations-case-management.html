Employee Relations Case Management

Investigation and case-management workflows supporting employee relations review, issue tracking, and resolution follow-up.

Objective

Improve consistency in how employee-relations issues are documented, escalated, reviewed, and resolved.

Context

Reflects work supporting employee-relations matters, disciplinary review, misconduct follow-up, and collaboration across HR and leadership functions.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • How are cases tracked from intake through closure?
  • Where do delays occur during review and follow-up?
  • What information must be documented for consistent handling?
  • How should cases be escalated when risk increases?

Methods Used

  • Case intake and categorization
  • Status tracking and aging review
  • Documentation standardization
  • Stakeholder coordination

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Average time to closure
  • Open case aging
  • Documentation completion rate
  • Repeat issue rate

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Human Resources
  • Employee relations teams
  • Managers and supervisors
  • Compliance stakeholders

Estimated Timeline

Typically 2–5 weeks depending on severity and follow-up needs.

Key Findings

  • Issue tracking was more reliable when categories and owners were standardized
  • Case aging improved when escalation thresholds were defined
  • Closure quality improved when evidence requirements were explicit

Recommendations

  • Use a centralized case log
  • Set escalation thresholds for aging matters
  • Require closure documentation for resolved cases

Skills Demonstrated

Case management, issue tracking, policy support, and employee-relations coordination.
