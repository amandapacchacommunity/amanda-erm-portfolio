Compliance Escalation Process

Escalation framework for routing significant compliance and conduct matters to the appropriate review and decision level.

Objective

Strengthen issue governance by creating a clearer escalation process for significant compliance concerns.

Context

Reflects governance and reporting work where issue severity, policy impact, and stakeholder sensitivity determined escalation level.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • Which issues require immediate escalation?
  • Who owns review and decision-making at each stage?
  • What routing rules reduce delay or confusion?
  • How should escalated issues be reported and tracked?

Methods Used

  • Severity-based routing review
  • Governance mapping
  • Escalation threshold definition
  • Status and reporting review

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Escalation turnaround time
  • Routing accuracy
  • Open high-severity issue aging
  • Leadership reporting timeliness

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Compliance teams
  • Executive leadership
  • HR and legal functions
  • Governance stakeholders

Estimated Timeline

Typically 2–4 weeks for escalation design and stakeholder alignment.

Key Findings

  • Issues moved faster when severity thresholds were documented clearly
  • Routing confusion decreased when owner roles were explicit
  • Leadership visibility improved with consistent escalation reporting

Recommendations

  • Use severity-based escalation criteria
  • Assign decision owners at each stage
  • Track escalated issues separately from general matters

Skills Demonstrated

Governance design, escalation planning, compliance routing, and issue oversight.
