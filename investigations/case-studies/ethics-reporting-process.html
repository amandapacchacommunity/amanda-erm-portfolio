Ethics Reporting Process

Ethics intake, triage, routing, and reporting workflow supporting timely response and leadership visibility.

Objective

Create a structured ethics reporting process that improves visibility, triage, escalation, and response consistency.

Context

Reflects compliance and investigative work involving issue intake, risk signaling, stakeholder coordination, and reporting to leadership.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • How are ethics concerns reported and categorized?
  • Which matters require rapid escalation?
  • What information is needed at intake?
  • How should trends be reported to leadership?

Methods Used

  • Intake framework design
  • Issue categorization
  • Escalation mapping
  • Trend reporting review

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Intake-to-review time
  • Escalation response time
  • Case categorization consistency
  • Reporting timeliness

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Compliance and ethics offices
  • Executive leadership
  • HR and legal functions
  • Operational managers

Estimated Timeline

Typically 2–4 weeks for process design and reporting structure alignment.

Key Findings

  • Issue intake improved when categories and thresholds were defined clearly
  • Escalation speed depended on clear routing rules
  • Leadership reporting became more useful when trends were grouped by theme

Recommendations

  • Standardize intake fields and issue categories
  • Define escalation thresholds by severity
  • Use regular themed reporting for leadership review

Skills Demonstrated

Ethics reporting design, compliance triage, escalation planning, and thematic reporting.
