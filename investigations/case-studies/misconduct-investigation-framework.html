Misconduct Investigation Framework

Structured approach to investigation intake, fact gathering, policy review, findings, and corrective action planning.

Objective

Create a consistent and defensible process for reviewing misconduct allegations and supporting institutional accountability.

Context

Reflects investigation and employee-relations work involving workplace misconduct, policy review, documentation, and collaboration with HR, legal, and leadership.

Questions That Informed the Work

  • What facts support or refute the allegation?
  • Which policies or conduct standards apply?
  • What documentation is needed for a defensible outcome?
  • What corrective actions reduce repeat risk?

Methods Used

  • Initial issue intake and triage
  • Interview and evidence review
  • Policy interpretation
  • Findings documentation
  • Corrective action analysis

KPIs / Measures Used

  • Case resolution timeline
  • Documentation completeness
  • Repeat incident rate
  • Escalation timeliness

Stakeholders / Teams Benefited

  • Human Resources
  • Legal and compliance teams
  • Executive leadership
  • People managers

Estimated Timeline

Typically 2–6 weeks depending on complexity and number of interviews.

Key Findings

  • Case handling improved when intake standards were defined
  • Policy interpretation needed to be documented consistently
  • Follow-up actions were stronger when owners and due dates were assigned

Recommendations

  • Use a standard case intake structure
  • Centralize evidence and documentation requirements
  • Track corrective action through closure

Skills Demonstrated

Investigative analysis, case documentation, policy enforcement, and stakeholder coordination.
